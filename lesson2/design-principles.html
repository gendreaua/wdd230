Fitt's Law

ccclib.org

Fitt's indicates that the efficiency of moving from one point to another on the screen. So if there is an eye-catching image or logo that is leading to the end result of a user clicking a button, how easy is it for the user to go from where they are looking to that button. This law also includes how easy it is or how far apart various poppular buttons or links are to make the site more user-friendly.

The library site has all of the important links near each other at the top. The login button is the first thing a person sees because it is bigger and eye-catching to make it easy for users to get to their library accounts. In close proximity to this button is the search link to find new titles and the location link to find the library near you. There is also a dropdown list of other options.

Reference: "Fitts Law Article: tutsplus.com "

PARC: Proximity

joann.com

PARC stands for Proximity, Alignment, Repetition, and Contrast. For Proximity: The user needs a visual cue on what items belong together. This is a way for related items to be seen as one.

Joann Fabrics utilizes this law by grouping similar items like yarns, fabrics, sales, regularly purchased items, and other options. In close proximity to each section are the buttons to shop those specific items.

Reference: "Proximity Article: webdesignerdepot.com "

Rule of Thirds

nerdcrafty.com

The rule of thirds is grid of equally shapped imaginary blocks sized 3 X 3, that makes the image visually appealing. This shows how the eye is viewing the image and helps a web-designer optimize their images.

The images on this site follow this rule very well. The top image has items on the left and right that are spaced out within the grid. As for the bottom image there are three soaps that are laid out in the bottom third of the image.

Reference: Rule of Thirds Article: tympanus.net"
